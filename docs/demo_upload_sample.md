# Inspection Report - Caustic Storage Tank

Report ID: IR-7C-2026-0721
Inspection date: 2026-07-21
Inspector: R. Alvarez
Area: Unit 7C

## Scope

Visual and instrumentation inspection of caustic storage tank `TK-77`, associated inlet isolation valve `V-512`, and level instrumentation following a scheduled quarterly integrity walkdown.

## Findings

- `TK-77` shell showed no visible corrosion or coating breakdown at the accessible lower course.
- Level transmitter on `TK-77` read 2% high against the manual strap reading taken during the walkdown.
- Handwheel on inlet isolation valve `V-512` required elevated torque to seat fully closed, consistent with packing wear.
- Secondary containment berm around `Storage Tank 77` was dry with no signs of past overflow.
- Operators on shift referred to the vessel as `Tank 77` and to the inlet valve as `Isolation Valve 512` during the walkdown.

## Recommended Actions

1. Raise a corrective work order to repack or replace the gland packing on `V-512` before the next fill cycle.
2. Recalibrate the level transmitter on `TK-77` against the manual strap reading within 30 days.
3. Confirm lockout/tagout points for `TK-77` and `V-512` are current per OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 before the next internal entry or valve service.
4. Add `TK-77` secondary containment to the weekly dry-berm checklist pending packing repair on `V-512`.

## Attachment Notes

- Condition aligns with maintenance log entry logged the same shift.
- No prior isolation procedure exists on file for `TK-77`; a LOTO procedure should be drafted referencing OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 and the Site Energy Isolation Standard EIS-04.
